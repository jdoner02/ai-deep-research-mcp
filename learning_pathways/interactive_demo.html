üìù Step 1: Understanding Your Question

When you ask a question, our AI first analyzes what type of question it is:

  • Academic: School project or scientific question
  • Technical: How something works
  • General: Everyday curiosity
  • Comparative: Comparing different things
# This is how the AI categorizes your question: query = "How do solar panels work?" query_type = analyze_query_type(query) # Result: "TECHNICAL" - because you're asking how something works!

üîç Step 2: Finding the Best Sources

Based on your question type, the AI chooses the best places to look for information:

  • Scientific questions: ArXiv research papers
  • General knowledge: Wikipedia and reliable websites
  • Technical topics: Academic journals and technical blogs
# The AI picks the right sources for your question: if query_type == "TECHNICAL": sources = ["wikipedia", "arxiv", "technical_websites"] elif query_type == "ACADEMIC": sources = ["google_scholar", "semantic_scholar"]

üìö Step 3: Gathering Information

The AI searches multiple sources simultaneously (like having many research assistants working at once!):

  • Extracts the most relevant information
  • Checks for accuracy and reliability
  • Organizes findings by importance
# Multiple searches happen at the same time: results = [] for source in sources: search_results = source.search(query) results.extend(search_results)
